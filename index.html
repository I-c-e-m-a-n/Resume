About me

I have always been a problem solver and regularly excelled in math with a strong analytical mindset. At the age of 14, I was finally able to put my skills to the test when I created my first computer program for a Lego Mindstorms robot. It required the robot to navigate an obstacle course for the First Lego League competition which was held in Singapore.

The competition sparked my interest in technology and since then, I have moved on to more structured projects like coding websites, creating video games, implementing data structures and algorithms, among other projects. I didn’t realize this when I was coding for the competition, but the first program I wrote was coincidently about implementing artificial intelligence.

Subsequently, I decided to pursue Artificial Intelligence as a part of my Computer Science major. My current interest lies in Neural Networks, more specifically Deep Learning and Reinforcement Learning.

There aren’t many fields that incite my passion as much as AI. Having already completed several courses externally from reputed universities like MIT and Stanford, I look forward to being able to put my acquired skills to the test.
